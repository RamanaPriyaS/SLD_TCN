How to Use SignLens

Alphabet Mode — Letter Detection

Hold a single ASL hand shape steady in front of your webcam. The progress bar fills as the model confirms the sign. When full, the letter is committed to the whiteboard.

  • SPACE — open flat palm facing camera
  • DEL — thumbs-down gesture removes last character
  • Keep your hand centred and well-lit for best accuracy
  • Hold each letter still for ~1 second to reach the stability threshold
→

Words Mode — Dynamic Sign Detection

Sign full words using natural ASL gestures. The model captures 30 consecutive frames of your hand, pose, and face and classifies the movement pattern.

  • Sign smoothly and continuously — movement is the key feature
  • A word commits after 4 consistent predictions ≥ 70% confidence
  • Brief pause between words resets the buffer
  • Trained on 34 words: hello, bird, elephant, will, refrigerator…

View Mode — Sign Skeleton Visualiser

Replay the actual dataset landmarks for any of the 34 trained words. Use this to study the correct hand shape and movement before practising yourself.

  • Pick a word from the dropdown → press ▶ Play
  • Drag the frame slider to scrub to any phase of the sign
  • Hit ⏸ Pause to freeze on a specific frame
  • Enable Loop for continuous playback
